Anand Srinivasan

I'm an avid researcher passionate about implementing scalable AI/ML solutions that make a real-world impact. My main focus has been on anomaly detection — a domain where subtle patterns and data deviations can signal critical insights in fields like cybersecurity, finance, and systems monitoring. Why Anomaly Detection and not other "hot" fields like NLP and Generative AI? Well I'm personally of the opinion that saving resources is as important as gaining them. And disastrous events that could end up being costly to the unprepared entity? Completely preventable! Ranging from projects in Malware analysis with GNNs an OCSVM embeddins to protect devices against zero day attacks to privacy preserving federated learning models for attestation of IoT devices, I have discovered different aspects of security and their importance. On the way, I also developed a novel cryptographic algorithm using geometry based approaches for resource constrained IoT devices, which is currently under patent review, and has potential use cases in spatial cryptography.

Here

What fuels my curiosity is not just the algorithms themselves, but how they intersect with human behavior and decision-making. That is, after all why we're here, is it not? To use ML methods as a tool to quickly understand patterns that we simply do not have the capability to compute on our own. This is why I believe in building systems that are not just accurate, but interpretable too. My interactions with my professors at BITS-Hyderabad has also left me informed of the dangerous potential AI could have in the wrong hands. And I am here to try and build ethical failsafes against such scenarios! I am currently involved in multiple projects, including recursive clustering based causality, hybrid anomaly detection frameworks and a neural distortion based interpretable anomaly detection framework. Looking forward to update it on my GIT soon!
